# PlantPulse Vision, Mission, and Goals (EBM)

This document defines the strategic hierarchy used by Evidence-Based Management (EBM):

- **Vision**: the intended long-term impact
- **Mission**: why PlantPulse is uniquely suited to deliver that impact
- **Goals**: measurable outcomes that show progress

Use this document as the "north star" and pair it with `docs/masterplan.md` for product details.

---

## Vision

Enable every batch-process facility to run calmer, safer, and more predictable operations through transparent, auditable, operator-first planning.

---

## Mission

Modernize legacy spreadsheet-and-slide scheduling workflows into a governed digital system that:

- preserves the wallboard mental model operators already trust
- improves planning quality through faster feedback and safer changes
- makes accountability explicit (shift ownership, approvals, audit trail)
- supports regulatory-ready operations without adding unnecessary friction

---

## Goals

Track goals at three levels so teams can connect strategy to execution.

### Strategic Goals (12-24 months)

1. Reduce schedule-disruption impact by improving planning resilience and visibility.
2. Establish PlantPulse as the default governed scheduling platform for multistep batch chains.
3. Demonstrate compliance-ready integrity (clear business audit history and role accountability).

### Intermediate Goals (quarterly)

1. Increase planner adoption of draft/propose/review workflows.
2. Improve operator confidence in wallboard accuracy and task clarity.
3. Reduce avoidable machine conflicts caused by timing, downtime, or handoff gaps.
4. Improve export/import reliability for Excel-based facility collaboration.

### Short-Term Goals (sprint-level experiments)

1. Validate one workflow improvement with users each sprint (e.g., chain wizard, filter ergonomics, conflict handling).
2. Shorten time-to-update after operational changes (downtime, shift exceptions, or stage edits).
3. Increase completion rate for critical in-app actions (task confirmation, plan proposal, commit review).

## Suggested EBM Mapping (KVAs)

These examples show where PlantPulse metrics can align to EBM Key Value Areas (KVAs):

- **Current Value (CV)**: user satisfaction with wallboard/planner usefulness, reduction in manual reconciliation work
- **Unrealized Value (UV)**: demand for enterprise workflow features (approvals, integrations, governance controls)
- **Time to Market (T2M)**: cycle time from identified scheduling pain point to shipped improvement
- **Ability to Innovate (A2I)**: speed and safety of testing new scheduling capabilities without regressions

Use these as a starting baseline; refine with real production telemetry and customer feedback.
